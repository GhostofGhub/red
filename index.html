Mors

[Translated from Polish] December 17, 1947 Incident Report: Missing Train Update Train expected to arrive at Warsaw station at midnight December 8 from the sanatorium, for further eastward travel, failed to meet its schedule, disappearing somewhere on the 24 mile train line between the sanatorium and Warsaw station amidst a heavy snowstorm. Delay initially assumed to be caused by temporary snow obstruction. However, repeated failure to establish radio contact and further failure of the train to arrive for the entirety of December 9 raised alarms, and a search team was deployed as soon as the weather allowed, late in the afternoon of December 10 from both the outskirts of Warsaw, following the track south-east. Train was discovered stationary, approximately 10 miles from the sanatorium at around 2200. All reported crew members were missing, despite no apparent damage to the locomotive itself, and coal stocks are still relatively high. The cargo itself, despite being in early stages of decomposition, was not disturbed when found.

[Translated from Morse Code] B7 - 75 TB [12 x 75] - 20-12-1947

Sixty years we cheated death and played gods, and as even Moscow and Stalingrad and Berlin became old victories, and all our enemies became old ghosts, we began to believe it ourselves - eventually we stopped counting altogether and the calendar remained frozen at 1949, even as the iron curtain was drawn shut, and the cold war went hot on four continents and new superpowers chased godhood of their own, we watched and laughed and drooled into our wines and cakes and sweetmeats, slumped limp in a drunken stupor over silken pillows, fondly reminding ourselves of past glories, dreaming ourselves still strong, convinced we were the sole owners of immortality, even as enemies gathered at the gates and the fountain ran dry.
